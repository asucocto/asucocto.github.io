What we do

The Office of the CTO was created in order to provide the general Berkeley community with access to more on-campus technology resources. Since our inception, we have been able to provide our fellow Bears with quality software products. You can see the products that we currently have in production below. Interested in seeing our schedule? Check out this calendar. Want to join our office? Check out our job opportunities to see what is available!

BearTransit Routes. Dining hall menus. Library hours. All in one application made for the busy UC Berkeley student.
BerkeleyTime

Project Hermione

Every bear has been a situation where an emergency prevents him or her from going to class. Many courses get their lectures recorded using Berkeley Educational Technology Services, but due to difficulty of setup and the costs for the university, the ASUC wanted to find an alternate solution. This is where Project Hermione comes in. Using state-of-the-art Computer Vision technology, our software developers were able to automate the lecture recording process so that it is easier for the administration to pay for Lecture Casting and easier for professors to set up the process. Check our our alpha demo here.

Berkeley Mobile

The Berkeley Mobile Application has a plethora features, including real-time BearTransit Routes, Dining hall menus (including breakfast, lunch, dinner, and late night menus for all dining halls), schedules for all of our public athletic facilities, and hours for every library on campus. We believe that the application is an absolute necessity for the busy Berkeley Student.

Want to get this application? You can download Berkeley Mobile for free for iOS here and for Android here.

BerkeleyTime

BerkeleyTime is a website that allows you to search the 8000+ courses from the Berkeley catalog and figure out what you're interested in. You can look up grade distributions and enrollment history for these classes and even explore the campus map to figure out where everything is. We believe that BerkeleyTime will change the way you do things at Berkeley. Visit the site by going here.
